Introduction

Distributed database

  • Blockchain = a distributed database
    • a list of linked records (blocks)
    • Represented by a hash tree where each block contains:
      • a cryptographic hash of the previous block
      • timestamp
      • transation data

Distributed Ledger

  • Blockchain is much more
    • a shared and a replicated transaction system
      • a transaction in a ledger
    • can be updated by applications (called smart contracts)
    • is kept synchronized through a collaborative process called consensus
  • Decentralized
    • it is replicated across many network participants
      • they collaborate in its maintenance
      • this supports real-world scenarios in exchaning goods and services
  • Append-only
    • once a transaction was added, it cannot be modified
    • guaranteed by cryptographic techniques
    • it is possible to determine the provenance of information

Distributed Ledger

Smart Contracts and Consensus

  • Smart contracts
    • a controlled access to ledger – modification, querying
    • allowing for a consistent update of information
  • Concensus
    • a process of keeping transactions synchronized across the network
Hyperledger Fabric

Hyperledger Fabric

  • Hyperledger project
    • Founded by Linux Foundation in 2015 to promote cross-industry blockchain technologies
    • collaborative approach to developing blockchain technologies via a community process
    • Hyperledger Fabric is one of the blockchain projects within Hyperledger
  • Hyperledger Fabric Difference
    • It is private and permissioned
      (permissionless system allows unknown identities to participate)
    • Participants enroll via a trusted Membership Service Provider (MSP)
    • Offers the ability to create channels
      • allowing a group of participants to create a separate ledger of transactions (participatns could be competitors and do not want other participants to see transactions)
      • If two participants form a channel, then those participants — and no others — have copies of the ledger for that channel
